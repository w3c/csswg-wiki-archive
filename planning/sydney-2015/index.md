</p><h4 id="visa-requirements">Visa Requirements</h4>
<p>
A visa is required for US Citizens to enter Australia (citizens of other countries please check with the appropriate authorities), a visa may be obtained electronically from the <a href="http://www.eta.immi.gov.au/" title="http://www.eta.immi.gov.au/" rel="noopener">Electronic Travel Authority web site</a>.
</p><h3 id="travel-tofrom-airport">Travel to/from airport</h3>
<p>
A taxi from the airport to the meeting venue or any of the listed hotels will cost between $30 and $50, depending on traffic. There&#039;s generally only a short wait to get a taxi, and a fairly orderly queue. This is probably the most convenient, and also the cheapest if you share with others…
</p>

<p>
If staying in Chinatown, the <a href="http://www.airportlink.com.au/directions/sydney-city.php" title="http://www.airportlink.com.au/directions/sydney-city.php" rel="noopener">airport train line</a> is reasonably convenient. A one-way ticket is $16.40. If you&#039;re staying in Pyrmont, you can still take the train, then jump on the <a href="http://www.sydneylightrail.transport.nsw.gov.au/" title="http://www.sydneylightrail.transport.nsw.gov.au/" rel="noopener">light rail</a> from &#039;Central&#039; to &#039;the Star&#039;. You&#039;ll need to pay $4.60 (in cash) after boarding the train.
</p>

<p>
Some hotels offer complimentary bus transfers to/from the airport - check with your hotel when booking.
</p><h3 id="hotels">Hotels</h3>
<p>
Please note that I don&#039;t have any direct experience of these hotels! If you&#039;ve stayed in one before (looking at you, Tab), chime in and recommend.
</p>

<p>
There are some close but expensive hotels:
</p>
<ul>
<li class="level1"><a href="http://www.1888hotel.com.au/" title="http://www.1888hotel.com.au/" rel="noopener">1888</a> has a reputation as being a great boutique hotel. Rooms seem to be ~$240 per night for stays of 2 nights or more.</li>
<li class="level1"><a href="http://www.novoteldarlingharbour.com.au/" title="http://www.novoteldarlingharbour.com.au/" rel="noopener">Novotel</a> is a reasonable international hotel. Rooms are ~$280 per night.</li>
<li class="level1"><a href="http://www.ibis.com/gb/hotel-1757-ibis-sydney-darling-harbour/index.shtml" title="http://www.ibis.com/gb/hotel-1757-ibis-sydney-darling-harbour/index.shtml" rel="noopener">Ibis</a> is also close by.</li>
</ul>

<p>
There are also a number of decent cheaper hotels in China town. These are further away (~20 minute walk from the office) - note that there is a light rail line from this area to the office. Services depart every 10 minutes during peak and a return ticket (which can be purchased on the train) is $6.40.
</p>
<ul>
<li class="level1"><a href="http://www.metrohotels.com.au/hotels/new-south-wales/aspire-hotel-sydney/welcome/" title="http://www.metrohotels.com.au/hotels/new-south-wales/aspire-hotel-sydney/welcome/" rel="noopener">Aspire</a> is about $130 a night.</li>
