<h1 id="tokyo-workshop-or-forum-2011">Tokyo Workshop or Forum 2011</h1>
<ul>
<li class="level1">2011 June 1</li>
<li class="level1">at <a href="http://www.krp.co.jp/english/" title="http://www.krp.co.jp/english/" rel="noopener">Kyoto Research Park</a>, Kyoto, Japan</li>
<li class="level1">Organizer: NTT/<abbr title="World Wide Web Consortium">W3C</abbr></li>
<li class="level1">See <a href="http://www.w3.org/Style/Japan-2011/" title="http://www.w3.org/Style/Japan-2011/" rel="noopener">http://www.w3.org/Style/Japan-2011/</a> for more up-to-date information.</li>
</ul><h2 id="meeting-minutes">Meeting Minutes</h2>
<ul>
<li class="level1"><a href="http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-style/2011Jun/0412.html" title="http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-style/2011Jun/0412.html" rel="noopener">Meeting Minutes</a></li>
</ul><h2 id="goal">Goal</h2>
<p>
The goal of this forum is providing an opportunity to <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr> community people and let them directly talk with the <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr> WG participants so that both the group participants and community people can have better mutual understanding and greater interest in each other&#039;s work.
Also we would like to see what kind of organization and/or mechanism would be suitable for improving the existing <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr> specifications for Asian layout.
</p><h2 id="scopeaudience">Scope / Audience</h2><h3 id="expected-participants">Expected Participants</h3>
<ol>
<li class="level1"><abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr>-WG participants</li>
<li class="level1">Stakeholders from related industries, e.g.:</li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li class="level1">Publishing or printing companies that own or seek solution for Asian text layout</li>
<li class="level1">Software vendors or open source projects that offer Asian text layout capability, e.g., Web browser vendors</li>
<li class="level1">Contents authors of Web pages and electronic books</li>
</ul>

<p>
Stakeholders from Asian countries who work for actual products are expected to participate in the forum.
</p><h2 id="topics">Topics</h2>
<p>
Possible agenda topics include, but are not limited to the following:
</p>
<ol>
<li class="level1">Spec review of CSS3 specifications which is related to Asian layout capability, e.g., Writing Modes and Text</li>
<li class="level1">Presentations by Asin layout implementers and device vendors</li>
<li class="level1">Presentations by contents authors, e.g., Web pages, publishing, printing, digital signage</li>
<li class="level1">Panel discussions by all the key stakeholders</li>
</ol>

<p>
Note: The requirements and opinions of the <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr> WG should be considered.
</p><h2 id="dates">Dates</h2>
<p>
Note: Right before or right after the <abbr title="Cascading Style Sheets">CSS</abbr> WG F2F meeting in Japan on June 1-3 is 
the candidate so far.
</p><h2 id="language">Language</h2>
